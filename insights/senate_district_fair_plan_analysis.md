# FAIR Plan Growth Mapped to CA Senate Districts (FY2021-FY2025)

*Analysis date: April 2025*

## Summary

California's FAIR Plan -- the insurer of last resort -- now covers **642,000 homes** as of FY2025, up **165%** since FY2021. Using Census population-weighted apportionment of county-level data, we mapped FAIR Plan policies-in-force and CDI total market data to all 40 State Senate districts.

FAIR Plan policies represent **7.4% of the total residential market** statewide -- up from 2.8% just four years ago (FY2025 FAIR Plan PIF against 2023 CDI total market, which has been essentially flat at ~8.6M policies).

The crisis is no longer confined to traditional wildfire zones. It is spreading from the Wildland-Urban Interface into suburban and urban communities across California -- and the January 2025 Palisades and Eaton fires will dramatically accelerate this trend in the districts already under the most pressure.

---

## Tier 1 -- High-Severity WUI Districts (Established Crisis)

These mountain, foothill, and rural districts sit squarely in the Wildland-Urban Interface, where homes are interspersed with or adjacent to fire-prone wildland vegetation. FAIR Plan penetration here is 5-16x the state average:

|----------|---------|-----------|-------------------|
| SD-4 | Alvarado-Gil (R) | 16.1% | 72,071 |
| SD-1 | Dahle (R) | 11.6% | 50,784 |
| SD-29 | Gomez Reyes (D) | 7.8% | 28,059 |
| SD-23 | Martinez Valladares (R) | 6.0% | 20,735 |
| SD-22 | Rubio (D) | 5.9% | 20,188 |
| SD-19 | Ochoa Bogh (R) | 5.1% | 25,128 |

SD-4 spans the Sierra Nevada foothills from Amador through El Dorado, Placer, and Calaveras counties -- the heart of California's WUI. One in six homeowners there is on the FAIR Plan. SD-1 covers the rural north where the Dixie and Carr fires drove mass voluntary market exits.

These districts contain suburban communities that border the WUI -- the "interface zone" where insurers are now pulling back preemptively. They started FY2021 with low FAIR Plan counts but are growing at 200-500%:

The Inland Empire (SD-31, SD-32) and central coast (SD-17, SD-18) are the new front lines. These communities may not think of themselves as wildfire country, but insurers increasingly treat them that way.

### January 2025 Fires: Palisades and Eaton

The districts covering the January 2025 fire zones were already under significant and growing FAIR Plan pressure *before* the fires:

| District | Senator | FAIR Share | FAIR PIF (FY2025) | 5yr Growth | Fire Zone |
|----------|---------|-----------|-------------------|-----------|-----------|
| SD-25 | Perez (D) | 5.0% | 16,794 | 86% | Eaton/Altadena |
| SD-27 | Stern (D) | 3.8% | 16,074 | 143% | Palisades |
| SD-24 | Allen (D) | 4.8% | 15,968 | 83% | Palisades |

**These numbers are pre-fire.** The FY2025 data ends September 30, 2024 -- three months before the Palisades and Eaton fires destroyed over 16,000 structures across Pacific Palisades, Altadena, and surrounding communities.

SD-25 (Perez) covers the Altadena foothills and Pasadena -- the Eaton fire's burn zone. The district already had a 5.0% FAIR Plan share, well above the state average. Thousands of homeowners who retained voluntary coverage before the fire will now face nonrenewals, pushing them onto the FAIR Plan or out of the market entirely.

SD-27 (Stern) and SD-24 (Allen) share the Palisades fire zone along the Santa Monica Mountains -- a classic WUI corridor where expensive homes meet steep, fire-prone chaparral. SD-27 had already seen 143% FAIR Plan growth even before the fires, driven by prior wildfire seasons and insurer pullbacks from Malibu, Calabasas, and Topanga.

The post-fire insurance shock in these districts will be severe. Historical precedent from the 2017-2018 North Bay and Camp fires shows FAIR Plan enrollment surging 2-3x in affected areas within 18-24 months of a major fire, as voluntary insurers exit en masse. Expect the combined FAIR Plan count across SD-24, SD-25, and SD-27 -- currently ~49,000 -- to climb significantly when FY2026 data becomes available.

## Tier 3 -- Urban and Coastal Districts (Emerging Contagion)

The most striking trend: FAIR Plan growth is now fastest in districts with historically minimal wildfire exposure. These urban and Bay Area districts saw 400-800% growth despite starting from low bases:

| District | Senator | 5yr Growth | FAIR PIF (FY2025) |
|----------|---------|-----------|-------------------|
| SD-9 | Grayson (D) | 831% | 9,991 |
| SD-7 | Arreguín (D) | 483% | 7,765 |
| SD-11 | Wiener (D) | 457% | 3,106 |
| SD-13 | Becker (D) | 471% | 4,297 |
| SD-15 | Cortese (D) | 405% | 3,298 |

This growth in urban cores signals that the crisis has moved beyond direct wildfire risk into a broader market failure -- insurers are repricing risk statewide, and homeowners in San Francisco (SD-11), the East Bay (SD-7), and Silicon Valley (SD-13, SD-15) are landing on the FAIR Plan for the first time. These are not WUI communities. The voluntary market is contracting everywhere.

## The Party Dynamic

Republican-held districts (10 seats) carry a **6.0%** average FAIR Plan share -- double the 2.9% in Democratic districts (30 seats). But the fastest growth is overwhelmingly in Democratic districts, where constituents are newly experiencing what rural communities have dealt with for years. This is rapidly becoming a bipartisan issue.

## Methodology

- **FAIR Plan PIF**: County-level policies-in-force from FAIR Plan quarterly reports (FY2021-FY2025, fiscal year ending September 30)
- **CDI total market**: County-level new + renewed policies from CDI Exposed County Analysis (2020-2023 calendar year). DIC (Difference in Conditions) policies excluded from totals as they are supplemental coverage attached to FAIR Plan policies, not independent coverage.
- **District apportionment**: County PIF apportioned to Senate districts using Census 2020 block-level population weights. Assumes FAIR Plan policies distribute proportionally to population within each county. In practice, policies concentrate near the WUI, so foothill and canyon communities within a district may have significantly higher FAIR Plan penetration than the district average suggests.
- **FAIR Plan share (7.4%)**: FY2025 FAIR Plan PIF (642,009) divided by 2023 CDI total market (8,623,352). Using cross-year comparison is reasonable because total residential PIF has been essentially flat (~8.6-8.8M) across 2020-2023.
